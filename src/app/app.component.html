Welcome to the Money Calculator.

What is this?

I was curious about how account balances can change over time, when you take into withdrawal rates. Most, if not all, similar calculators that I found online focused primarily on total balance at a certain age, assuming a certain yield, with no way to take into account regular withdrawals. This website is not meant to be financial advice and I do not guarantee that any numbers outputted are accurate.

Naming the Scenario

We'd define a scenario as the specific numerical values (initial balance, starting age, ending age, etc) that is entered into the form. In order to help you differentiate between different scenarios (should you choose to use add multiple for comparisons), you can name each scenario that you enter, provided that the name isn't too long. You can use names of up to 20 characters, including spaces, numbers, and letters. Go crazy.

Initial Balance

The initial balance should be whatever number you want the program to start with - this number can be as low as zero to as high as $1,000,000 (or more). In other words, this is the number that represents the balance of your theoretical account.

Starting Age

This is the age at which you want to start counting the growth of your money. Starting age and Ending Age are constant once submitted. To enter new Starting/Ending Ages, you must clear the graph.

Ending Age

This is the age at which you want us to stop counting the growth of your money. After all, the graph has to end somewhere! Also: Starting age and Ending Age are constant once submitted. To enter new Starting/Ending Ages, you must clear the graph.

Age to Use Money

This is the age at which you want to start doing withdrawals from your account. If you don't want the effect of withdrawals to drastically impact your outcome, you can set this age equal to your Ending Age and set withdrawal amount to $1. Also, tiny quirk: if you want more accuracy, the Age to Use Money should be a multiple of Math.floor(stopAge / startAge).

How Much Money

This is the dollar amount that you wish to withdraw per month, starting from the age in which you would like to start using your money. This could be useful especially when you'd like to see if your current retirement goals can realistically last you throughout the rest of your life. The withdrawal is calculated at the start of the month, and interest is applied subsequently.

Expected Gain

This is the average percentage that you expect to earn in your account in the span of a year. We assume that it compounds monthly. For example, a savings account would probably earn somewhere around 1%, while investments in the S&P 500 could yield around 8% (on average).
